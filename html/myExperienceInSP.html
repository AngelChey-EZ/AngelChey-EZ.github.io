About My School

About SP

Singapore Polytechnic was set up in 1954, and it is the first polytechnic to be established in Singapore. SP offers courses across a wide variety of industries, includes ... architecture and the built environment, business, computing, sciences, engineering, life skills & communication, mathematics and science, media, arts & design, mechanical and aeronautical engineering, maritime academy,with over 220 000 graduates.

SP may be the oldest Polytechnic, but the pioneering spirit is kept very much alive. SP ensures its builiding and facilities are always up-to-date and well-equipped for training students in the new industries and emerging technologies.

About SOC

SP's school of computing is equipped with more than 950 Windows and Macintosh workstations in our studios, to support the different training requirements ... of our diploma courses. These workstations are equipped with application software, popular programming/development tools, as well as hardware like DVD writers, and media card readers, etc. Our studios are also equipped with peripheral devices such as scanners, colour printers, multimedia accessories, and are linked via Local Area Networks running on Windows and TCP/IP.

SP's SOC is prepared to train students in the industry of computing tachnology.

Diploma in Applied AI and Analytics (DAAA), is designed with a useful and relevant curriculum that imparts students with important skills to contribute to the workforce in the AI and Analytics domain.

DCITP  ≪--click me to see more about the course    >>click me to learn more

Common ICT programme (DCITP) is best suited for students who are passionate about Information Technology (IT) but undecided about which IT course to take. This course is designed to give students a broad introduction to the Info comm landscape and help them gain insights into the occupations and job roles, career pathways and employment opportunities in the various Info comm sectors. DCITP helps students to build a strong foundation in programming and IT fundamental. When semester 1 of year 1 had ended, students will need to choose either DAAA, DISM or DIT.

Diploma in Infocomm Security Management (DISM) gives students the competitive edge for a boost in their future studies and career. Its interesting curriculum covers offensive attacks, defensive methods and investigative skills to keep the cyber world safe. The DISM curriculum covers the important areas of security technology, security management, information technology, law and teamwork and communications.

DIT    ≪--click me to see more about the course    >>click me to learn more

Diploma in Information Technology (DIT) is positioned to focus on Software & Applications development with three specialist tracks, Software Development, User Experience (UX) and Immersive Simulation. The course work closely with industry leaders when designing their pedagogy, and in building learning facilities. Their students will have opportunities to work with leading industry companies such as Microsoft, Accenture, GovTech, and others more through internship opportunities and project collaborations.

What am I studying

I am currently at year 1 semester 1, and I am learning 6 modules. These modules include Communicating for Project (Proposal) Effectiveness, Critical and Analytical Thinking (CAT), Mathematics, Education and Career Guidance 1: Personal Development, Front-End Web Development, Fundamentals of Programming, Fundamentals of Computing. From these modules, I will improve my communication skills, learn computing maths, explore different careers and further studies path, learn how to create a web page from scratch, learn how a computer work and learn the fundamental of programming.

Enrichment Programme

SP had conducted many enrichment programmes for students to participant. These programmes are usually focuses on areas like healthy lifestyle, mental well-being, coping with stress and study tips. Some of the programme that I have attended is the “Level Up your relational and emotional game”, “Sugar Addict Talk”, “Learn how to play tennis”, “How to ACE your FOP” and others more. These programme allows me to learn how to take care of myself better and also gain some knowledge that is not taught by school.


Competition

SP provides us many competition opportunities to showcase our skills and talent. This allows us to explore the world outside class, and compete against many other teenagers age like us. Participating competition could build up our portfolio, and also grow and learn new things during the competition. I have not participated any competition yet.
